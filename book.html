InterMISSION: Nike, and the Quest for the Tater Tot

One day, Saint Nike was really hungry. The good news was that it was lunch, but she didn't have a lunch. No one, of course, would share their lunch because they needed to eat too (and the fact that she was Nike didn't help). Underneath her masquerade of innocence, she really really, wanted to eat somebody. Nike envisioned within her mind a feast of the flesh of the other Saints, which would not only satisfy her craving for food, but also make her throw up. Thus, she would be hungry again and continue to eat. Because she would have eaten more than what was necessary, she would have been committing the sin of gluttony... which Nike soon realized that God wouldn't approve.

But there was indeed salvation for her sinful thoughts. It was called, The HUB (Holy Union Building). And so God sent Phanuel, angel of repentance and hope, to tell Saint Brock to take Nike to the HUB so that Nike's craving for food would be satisfied.

And so it came to pass, that they arrived at on of the fast food burger place within the HUB. Nike ordered the chicken tender meal, while Brock ordered a bag of French fries. All was saved and you, avid reader, may stop reading. But one does not simply get redeemed by God with a chicken tender meal. Nike's gluttonous thoughts may have been purged, but God would have to test her first.

And so it came to pass, that Brock opened his paper bag, and found that it was not a bag of French fries, but a bag of French fries... and a tater tot. Now, Nike had OCD, and could not control the imperfect, unbalanced symmetry of this terrible nightmare. She quickly vacuumed the tainted entity of the tater tot. The first test was failed, for she had stolen Brock's food!

Days went by, and Nike and Brock continued to buy chicken tender meals and French fries (respectively), some days had an annoying tater tot. Other days did not. Brock recorded days that had a tater tot with a 1, and days with no tater tots with a 0. He looked at his chart one day and this was what he received: 1-0-0-1-0-0-1

A History of Trolls:

Sometime in the 1800's in the U.S, a story arose about three goats attempting to cross a bridge. Now, after modern historians have inspected and analyzed the story by close reading, they declared that story a fabrication to cover up the truth. After interviewing one of the greatest trolls, here's what the actual story is:

Hundreds of years ago, there were some trolls that lived under an unnamed bridge somewhere in the U.S. One day, a saint, known specifically as Virgin Mary, walked too close to the bridge. This Saint Mary owned a small, fluffy lamb, whom the trolls were instantly attracted to, in a non-romantic way. One of these trolls was extremely rich, and offered the innocent Mary $9,001 to bribe her into giving the little lamb to him. Saint Mary, being the young, innocent girl she was, accepted this bribe and handed over the little lamb to the troll, who salivated in delight. However, this Mary did not realize that she had just been trolled since the money was fake! It was too late though; by the time Mary had returned to the bridge to claim back her lamb, the trolls had already eaten it, bones and all. The trolls, unknowingly, had consumed a holy lamb that transformed their appearances into that of a human’s, and were able to disperse throughout human society and live undetected up to this day.

Today, in a certain city called Seattle in the most North-Western corner of the U.S, there live a group of trolls under the 420 bridge within the campus of the U.W (Unified Waggery) under the cover of TSers (Transitional Saints) and EEPers (Everlasting, Enshrined Protectors). In the year of 2015 C.E, there happened to be a divine prophet by the name of Lil' D amidst the 18 TSers that year. Since these saints were cooped up in a tiny room for six hours a day learning about life, language, history of other species, a bunch of jumbled up letters/numbers/symbols, and restoration, by the time lunch rolled around, these saints were extremely hungry and longed for a bite. Now, though the saints have long evolved from their previous troll forms, many of them still possessed a gene that activated their desire for meat whenever the hunger became unbearable. With the exception of Saint Jordan, who had abstained from eating meat for so long that he was now immune to the effects of this gene, all the other TSers longed for a bite of the savory little lamb that they once consumed whenever driven to starvation.
